german army

infantry weapons

karabiner 98k

K98k is a bolt-action rifle developed by Mauser, was the primary German service rifle during World War II. Noted for its reliability and effective range, it influenced subsequent firearm designs. Despite being replaced by semi-automatic and automatic rifles, it remains in use in conflicts worldwide.

Gewehr 43

G43 is a semi-automatic rifle developed by Germany during World War II. It was designed based on the Soviet SVT-40 and the earlier G41. Despite initial reliability issues, it was produced from 1943 to 1945 in large numbers and used extensively by the German army.

watch video

Maschinenpistole 40

MP 40 designed by Heinrich Vollmer, was a submachine gun used extensively by the Axis powers during World War II. Known for its modern features and reliability, it influenced post-war firearm designs, produced from 1943 to present and remains popular in various parts of the world.

watch video

Sturmgewehr 44

StG 44 was the world’s first assault rifle, developed by Hugo Schmeisser during World War II. Its design greatly influenced modern firearms, including the AK-47. It was used extensively by the German army, marking a significant shift in infantry weapon technology.

watch video

Maschinengewehr 42

MG 42 is a German general-purpose machine gun, was introduced in 1942 during World War II. Known for its high firing rate and reliability, produced from 1942 to present it used the standard 7.92×57mm Mauser rifle round. Its unique sound led to the nickname "Hitler’s buzzsaw".

watch video

Luger P08

Luger also known as the Parabellum-Pistole or Luger P08, is a semi-automatic pistol designed by Georg Luger. Produced from 1898 to 1949, it was used by several nations. It’s known for its toggle-locked recoil operation. Today, Luger pistols and accessories are sought after by collectors.

watch video

Panzerschreck 43

Panzerschreck 43 was an 88mm reusable anti-tank rocket launcher developed by Germany during World War II. It was designed as a lightweight infantry anti-tank weapon and was an enlarged copy of the American bazooka. The weapon was shoulder-launched and fired a fin-stabilized rocket.

heavy weapons

Pak 40

Pak 40, a German anti-tank gun, was a significant weapon during World War II. Developed between 1939-1941, it entered service in 19421. With 23,303 units produced, it formed the backbone of German anti-tank artillery. It was capable of firing at a range of up to 7,678 meters.

Flak 38

Flak 38, an improved version of Flak 30, was a 20mm anti-aircraft gun used by Germany during World War II. It was the primary German light anti-aircraft gun and the most numerously produced German artillery piece throughout the war. Variants included Gebirgsflak and Flakvierling.

Sd.Kfz. 124 Wespe

Sd.Kfz. 124 Wespe, also known as Leichte Feldhaubitze 18/2 auf Fahrgestell Panzerkampfwagen II (Sf.), was a German self-propelled gun used during World War II. It was based on a modified Panzer II chassis. The Wespe was in production from February 1943 until June 1944. A total of 676 were produced.

flak gun

Flak 88, officially known as the 8.8 cm Flak 18/36/37/41, was a German 88 mm anti-aircraft and anti-tank artillery gun developed in the 1930s and produced from 1933 to 1945. It was widely used by Germany throughout World War II and is one of the most recognized German weapons of the conflict

leFH 18

The 10.5 cm leFH 18, a German light howitzer used in World War II, was the standard artillery piece of the Wehrmacht. Adopted for service in 1935, it was used by all divisions and artillery battalions. From 1935 to the end of the war, 11,848 were produced, along with 10,265 of the leFH 18/40 variant.

le.IG 18

7.5cm le.IG 18 was an infantry support gun, was used by the German Wehrmacht during World War II. Developed by Rheinmetall between 1927 and 1932 and produced from 1932 to 1945, it fired a 75mm high explosive shell up to 4,000 meters. It was mobile, effective, and remained in service throughout the war.

Panzer IV

Panzer IV was developed in the late 1930s, it was a German medium tank extensively used during World War II. It was the most numerous German tank, Produced from 1936 to 1945 with about 8,553 units built. Originally designed for infantry support, it evolved to combat armored vehicles, adapting to face formidable tanks like the T-34.

Sturmgeschütz III

StuG III was a German assault gun produced during World War II. It was the most-produced fully tracked armoured fighting vehicle, and the second-most produced German armored combat vehicle. It was initially intended as a mobile assault gun for direct-fire support for infantry, but was later employed as a tank destroyer.

Panther V

Panther V officially known as Panzerkampfwagen V Panther, was a German medium tank used during World War II. It was designed to counter the Soviet T-34 and replace the Panzer III and IV. Despite its impressive firepower, protection, and mobility, its reliability was less impressive. Produced from 1943 to 1945 Approximately 6,000 units were produced.

Tiger I

Tiger I officially known as Panzerkampfwagen VI Tiger Ausf. E, was a German heavy tank used in World War II. it began service in 1942, Produced from 1942 to 1944. Known for its powerful 8.8 cm KwK 36 gun and robust armor, it was a formidable adversary on the battlefield and played a significant role in German armored warfare.

Ostwind

Flakpanzer IV was a German self-propelled anti-aircraft gun developed in 1944. Based on the Panzer IV tank, it featured an open-top, hexagonal turret housing a 3.7 cm FlaK 43. It was effective against light vehicles and minor fortifications, and despite its open-top design, it offered better armor and firepower than its predecessor.

Panzerwerfer 42

Panzerwerfer 42, a self-propelled multiple rocket launcher, was used by Nazi Germany during World War II. Based on the Opel Maultier half-track, it was equipped with a 15 cm Nebelwerfer 41 launcher. It was effective for large-scale rocket barrages, covering large areas and adding psychological elements to the fight.

SdKfz 222

SdKfz 222 Scout Car, a light armored reconnaissance vehicle, was produced by Germany from 1935 to 1944. It was equipped with a 2 cm KwK 30 L/55 autocannon and a Maschinengewehr 34. Known for its speed and effective range, it excelled in areas with good road networks. played a crucial role in gathering intelligence on the battlefield.

SdKfz 251

SdKfz 251 half-track designed by Hanomag, was a World War II German armored personnel carrier Introduced in the late 1930s. It was based on the unarmored Sd.Kfz. was used to transport the Panzergrenadier (German mechanized infantry) into battle. With over 15,000 units produced, it was the most widely produced German half-track of the war
